Moroccan Sports

Passion, pride, and achievement in Moroccan athletics.

Athletic Spirit

Sports in Morocco

Sports play an integral role in Moroccan culture and society, with football (soccer) being by far the most popular sport. The country has a rich sporting tradition that extends beyond football to include athletics, tennis, golf, martial arts, and traditional sports like "Tbourida" (traditional horse riding performance).

Morocco has made significant investments in sports infrastructure over the past decades, building modern stadiums, training facilities, and hosting international competitions. These investments reflect the country's commitment to developing sports at all levels, from grassroots to professional.

Other popular sports include kickboxing, basketball, tennis, and equestrian sports. The country also supports youth development and has invested in modern sports facilities and international events, such as hosting the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations.

The country has achieved notable success in international competitions, especially in football, athletics, and combat sports, with Moroccan athletes gaining worldwide recognition for their achievements.

The Beautiful Game

Football in Morocco

Football is undeniably Morocco’s most popular sport, uniting fans nationwide with deep passion. The national team, known as "The Atlas Lions," has proudly represented Morocco on the global stage, making history as the first African nation to reach the FIFA World Cup semi-finals in Qatar 2022.

Major Clubs

Wydad Athletic Club

Founded in 1937 in Casablanca, Wydad AC is one of Morocco’s and Africa’s most successful football clubs, boasting multiple CAF Champions League titles and numerous national championships.

Wydad Athletic Club (Wydad AC) will compete in the 2025 FIFA Club World Cup held across 12 U.S. cities from June 14 to July 13, 2025. They qualified by winning the 2021–22 CAF Champions League. Placed in Group G with Manchester City, Juventus, and Al Ain, Wydad will open against Manchester City on June 18 in Philadelphia. This will be their third Club World Cup appearance. The expanded 32-team format highlights the tournament’s global growth, with matches broadcast worldwide.

Raja Club Athletic

Raja Casablanca, another iconic club from the city, boasts a rich history of domestic and continental success. Renowned for its passionate fanbase, Raja shares a fierce and historic rivalry with Wydad AC.

Raja has also competed in the FIFA Club World Cup, representing African football on the international stage. The club continues to develop talent and maintain a strong presence in continental tournaments, aiming to enhance Morocco’s reputation in global football.

AS FAR

AS FAR, based in Rabat, is one of Morocco’s oldest and most prestigious football clubs, with a rich history dating back to 1958. Known as the “Military Club,” AS FAR has won numerous Botola league titles and Moroccan Throne Cups, as well as the CAF Champions League in 1985. The club has a strong fan base and is famous for its disciplined style of play and contribution to Moroccan football development.

AS FAR regularly competes in continental tournaments and represents Morocco with pride on the African and international football stages. The club continues to focus on nurturing young talent and maintaining its status among Africa’s elite football teams.

The National Team

The Moroccan national football team, nicknamed "The Atlas Lions," has represented the country with distinction in international competitions. Their journey has been marked by historical achievements:

  • First African team to qualify for the World Cup knockout stage (1986)
  • First African and Arab team to reach World Cup semi-finals (2022)
  • African Cup of Nations champions in 1976
  • Multiple qualifications for the FIFA World Cup

The team has produced many talented players who have gone on to play for top clubs in Europe, serving as ambassadors for Moroccan football on the world stage.

Beyond Football

Other Popular Sports

Athletics

Morocco has a strong tradition in middle and long-distance running, with legendary athletes like Hicham El Guerrouj, who holds world records in the mile and 1500 meters.

Nawal El Moutawakel, a trailblazer for women in Moroccan and Arab sports, became the first Moroccan and Arab woman to win Olympic gold when she triumphed in the 400m hurdles at the same 1984 Olympics. Beyond her athletic success, she has been a prominent sports administrator and advocate, inspiring countless female athletes across the Arab world.

aid Aouita is one of Morocco’s most celebrated middle- and long-distance runners, renowned for his Olympic gold medal in the 5,000 meters at the 1984 Los Angeles Games. During the 1980s, he set multiple world records in events including the 1,500m, 2,000m, and 3,000m, solidifying his place as one of the greatest runners in history.

Tennis

Tennis has grown in popularity in Morocco, with the country hosting ATP and WTA tournaments. Moroccan players have made their mark on the international circuit.

Younes El Aynaoui : One of Morocco’s top tennis stars, reached ATP No. 14 in 2003. Known for his strong serve and deep Grand Slam runs, including two Australian Open quarterfinals. His legendary five-set battle with Andy Roddick in 2003 is among the longest matches in tournament history.

Combat Sports

Boxing is one of Morocco’s most established combat sports, with a rich Olympic history and numerous national clubs. Moroccan boxers have represented the country in international competitions, earning medals and recognition across Africa and beyond.

Muay Thai and kickboxing have surged in popularity in Morocco. The country has produced top-level fighters, most notably Badr Hari, a global star in the sport. These disciplines are supported by dedicated federations and are widely practiced across Moroccan cities.

Personal Connection

My Sports Journey

Growing up in Morocco, sports—especially football—played a significant role in my life. Like most Moroccan children, I spent countless hours playing football in neighborhood streets and watching matches with family and friends.

I've been a passionate supporter of Wydad_Athletic_Club since childhood, attending matches at the stadium whenever possible. The electric atmosphere, chanting fans, and collective spirit made these experiences unforgettable.

The 2022 World Cup was particularly special for me as I watched with pride as our national team made history by reaching the semi-finals. This achievement wasn't just about football—it united Moroccans across the globe and showcased our country's talent and determination to the world.

My sports journey began at a young age, fueled by passion and discipline. One of my proudest moments was becoming the Kickboxing Champion of Morocco in 2013, when I was just 12 years old.

That victory marked the beginning of my deep commitment to combat sports. Training hard and competing taught me resilience, focus, and the value of perseverance—qualities that continue to guide me in all aspects of life.
